Huddle

A travel app for groups of travellers who want to stay organized and efficient during trips.

"

Problem

Users travelling in groups are often plagued by indecisiveness and miscommunication. Although numerous travel apps exist on the market, most aren't optimized for group trips and none are able to provide opportunities for real-time collaboration.

Solution

Huddle is an app that enables users to create itineraries and share them with other travellers. Once an invite has been sent and accepted, users can easily view and edit their shared schedules. Any edits made are synchronized across the entire group.

Research

I conducted five interviews over Zoom to understand how people felt about planning trips and what aspects of planning frustrated them the most. My three main takeaways from the interviews were:
1. Users often encounter issues on group trips because differing interests would lead to plans being changed or abandoned.
2. Most people find planning trips to be tiring and overwhelming.
3. Most people like to have some degree of structure to their travel plans, even people who are more spontaneous. 
I summarized all the ideas gleaned from the interviews into an affinity diagram, pictured here on the right. 

Ideation


Name & Logo

I was determined to create an app that could address the problems brought up in the interviews concerning issues with planning group trips. I named my app "Huddle", from the term "group huddle", in order to emphasize the app's goal of fostering communication and connection. The logo, while abstract, lightly evokes the image of four heads coming together in a huddle.

Testing

Guerilla Testing

I used the guerilla testing method to see how well people could navigate through the app without my assistance by giving each tester four tasks:

1. Sign up with Huddle using your email
2. Create a new itinerary
3. Add a collaborator to your new itinerary
4. Book a hotel through the app and add it to your itinerary

I summarized my observations in the image to the right. The area in need of the most improvement was that some users' first instinct was to go to the search bar to create a new trip, and not the trips page. I iterated the app one more time with these observations in mind.
